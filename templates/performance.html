The Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) is a measure of the prediction error in a regression model. It quantifies the average difference between the predicted values and the actual values. In the context of Apple stock prediction, the lower RMSE for the test set compared to the training set indicates that the model's performance is better on unseen data.

This difference can be attributed to the model's ability to generalize well to new data. During training, the model learns from the historical stock price data, capturing patterns and relationships. However, during testing, the model is evaluated on unseen data, simulating real-world scenarios where it needs to make predictions on future stock prices.

The lower test RMSE suggests that the model has successfully learned the underlying patterns in the data and can make accurate predictions on unseen instances. It indicates that the model has captured the essential trends and dependencies in the Apple stock prices, allowing it to generalize well to new market conditions.
